<span style="font-size: 15px !important; color: #BD0026;">June 2020 </span>

<span style="font-size: 18px !important; color: #07153D;">Eunsang Cho presented his recent research at <a href="http://depts.washington.edu/sinter/">SINTER</a> (Snow International) meeting on 5th June! He was invited As an early career scientists, it was such a honor, and great opportunity to share his research with the international snow science community. The presentation title is <b><i>"The rediscovery of the value of 40-years airborne gamma radiation SWE”"</i></b> &nbsp; SINTER (formerly i-SWGR) is an international collective of scientists with interest in remote sensing of snow, supported by ground-based observations, physical modeling, and data assimilation. SINTER operates distinctly from other community-wide efforts which aim to coordinate progress on snow science, such as the NASA Terrestrial Hydrology Program, monthly NASA SnowEx online meetings, Global Cryosphere Watch Snow Watch working group, etc.</span>
